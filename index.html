Experiential Psychodynamic Psychotherapy

A private practice focused on deep emotional understanding, relational insight, and meaningful personal change.

About This Practice

This private practice offers experiential psychodynamic psychotherapy—an approach that emphasizes lived emotional experience, unconscious patterns, and the therapeutic relationship as a pathway to lasting growth.

The focus is not on quick fixes or surface-level techniques, but on developing deeper self-understanding and the capacity for more authentic relating.

What to Expect in Therapy

Sessions provide a safe, confidential space to explore your inner world. Together we will:

  • Pay close attention to feelings as they emerge in the present moment
  • Explore recurring patterns in relationships, both past and current
  • Identify how early experiences may quietly shape present emotions and behaviors
  • Work collaboratively to uncover underlying emotional meanings
  • Support the gradual emergence of greater self-awareness and emotional freedom

Meaningful change unfolds naturally through insight and new ways of experiencing oneself and others.

The Therapeutic Approach

This practice is grounded in contemporary psychodynamic principles, including the empirically supported framework described by Jonathan Shedler. The therapeutic relationship itself becomes a vital space for understanding and growth.

Sessions are typically 50 minutes long and held weekly (or more frequently when helpful).
