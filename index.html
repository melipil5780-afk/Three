Experiential Psychodynamic Psychotherapy

A private practice dedicated to deep emotional exploration, insight, and meaningful change.

About This Practice

This practice offers experiential psychodynamic psychotherapy, a depth-oriented approach that emphasizes the exploration of unconscious patterns, emotional experience, and relational dynamics.

Rather than focusing on quick symptom reduction alone, the work here centers on understanding the underlying emotional meanings and interpersonal patterns that shape how we feel, think, and relate to others.

The therapeutic process is collaborative, respectful, and attuned to your unique experience.

What to Expect

In sessions, we will:

  • Explore your feelings and thoughts as they arise in the moment
  • Attend to patterns in your relationships, past and present
  • Examine how earlier life experiences may continue to influence current emotional life
  • Work together to recognize and understand recurring emotional themes
  • Foster greater self-awareness, emotional freedom, and capacity for meaningful connection

Change emerges gradually through deepened understanding and new ways of experiencing oneself and others.

The Approach

This practice draws heavily from contemporary psychodynamic understanding, including the empirically supported framework outlined by Jonathan Shedler. The focus is on the therapeutic relationship as a vehicle for insight and growth, emphasizing emotional experience over technique alone.

Sessions are typically 50 minutes, held weekly or more frequently as needed.
